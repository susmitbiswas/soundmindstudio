There's something about performing live that no rehearsal can fully prepare you for — the lights, the crowd, the electricity in the air. But when you've spent two months building something with a group of people who feel like family, you step onto that stage with something even more powerful: absolute trust in the people standing beside you.

## The Two Months Before

Our journey to the stage started quietly — Sunday gatherings that became the heartbeat of this whole experience. Week after week, we showed up, instruments in hand, voices warmed up, ready to build something together. As the performance date drew closer, once a week wasn't enough. We started meeting several times a week, pushing ourselves, tightening every note, every transition, every beat.

But here's the thing — the rehearsals were never just rehearsals.

Every single gathering, someone would volunteer to bring snacks and drinks. Nobody asked. Nobody needed to. People just showed up with food, with laughter, with stories. We'd take breaks and somehow end up in the most genuine conversations — swapping life stories, cracking jokes, learning who everyone really was beyond the music. I found myself looking forward to those moments just as much as the music itself.

This group had something special: incredible instrument players, gifted musicians, powerhouse vocalists — and above all, genuinely good people. The kind of people you don't just perform with; the kind you actually want in your corner. I already know some of these friendships will last a lifetime.

## The Big Day — February 15

The festival itself was a five-hour celebration of nonstop live performance. Five hours. Of pure entertainment, back to back, community and culture pouring off the stage the entire time.

Our group performed the **folk segment** at the Bay Area Bengali Music Festival — 45 full minutes on that stage.

Oh boy, what energy.

We danced. We sang. We gave everything we had, and the crowd gave it right back. There's a kind of joy that only happens when a group of people who truly love what they're doing perform together — and I felt it in every single moment we were up there. The folk segment was alive in a way I struggle to put into words. You just had to be there.

## Why I'd Do It All Over Again

Looking back, it wasn't really about the 45 minutes on stage. It was about the two months that made those 45 minutes possible — the Sundays, the late-week sessions, the snacks, the laughter, the stories shared in between songs.

Music brought us together, but it was the people that made it unforgettable.

If I ever get the chance to do this again with this team, the answer is yes. Without hesitation.
